Boba Glitched 3: Advanced Glitch & Layering in Photoshop

Project Overview

The final installment in the Boba Glitched series transitions from raw data manipulation to deliberate layered glitch creation in Adobe Photoshop. This work uses RGB channel isolation and halftone filters to build a structured digital distortion aesthetic.

Category:

Advanced Digital Art / Compositing

Tools Used:

Adobe Photoshop

Focus:

RGB Separation & Halftone Effects

Process Breakdown

  1. Imported the image into Photoshop and cleared the background to black.
  2. Created two masked copies of the boba image layer.
  3. Unlinked color channels on each copy:
    • First copy: unchecked Red and Green (kept Blue only)
    • Second copy: unchecked Blue and Green (kept Red only)
  4. Shifted the colored layers slightly to create RGB glitch separation.
  5. Applied the Halftone Pattern filter:
    • Pattern Type: Line
    • Size: 2
    • Contrast: 5
  6. Adjusted tones using Curves with a Smart Object layer for flexibility.
  7. Used the Lasso Tool to isolate rectangles, duplicating and shifting them to produce vertical glitching and layered distortion.
